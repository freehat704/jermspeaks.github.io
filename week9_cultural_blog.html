Revisiting Learning Style

Date: June 20th, 2014

From the pre-prep work, I found out I have an abstract random learning style based on the evaluation we took. This means I tend to be people-oriented, my thinking process is based more on feelings and intuition, and I would rather be in a cooperative environment rather than working alone. At DBC, with pairing sessions, this sounds great because I think what my learning style says is I'll be empathetic and enjoy working with others.

The downsides are finding it hard to take criticism, working in a restrictive environment, and focusing on one thing at a time. At DBC, we have already been training with feedback, so I will possibly struggle more with that. I don't mind creating an MVP for programming because it's the most practical and pragmatic solution we can come up with, although I may need reminders later. I found that I focus better in chunks of time, which is why I've been using things like the Pomodoro technique.

From the looks of it, my plan of action is not to take much action and go out of feeling because that's what most comfortable for me. That means the constant feedback I get will be internalized and tried to be understood more. With a growth mindset, I'm trying to go more for a learning conversation and a learning attitude with the things I do at DBC, and embrace what's going to come to me rather than have an instant rejection. Try once and evaluate. I'm looking forward to DBC next week!
