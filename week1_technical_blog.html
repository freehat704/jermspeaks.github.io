Analysis of 3 sites from UX and User Design POV

NPR Music: I'm using the same sites from my wireframe websites. This website blares out on the left the main content while on the right is reserved for additional content you can browse later. The navigation buttons are large and easy to read, allowing you to skip to other websites if you don't like this one.


Metafilter: It's a lot of text when you get on this site at first. But they're mainly just links to other websites. The titles are very appealing, which is a testament to the community's creativity. There is a lot of curated content to go through, and if you feel overwhelmed, there's a search bar to see if there's something your interested in from the get go. There's no one telling you how to use the website.


Kickstarter: The user opens the page and find this large promotional content. It's not overwhelming at all and scrolling down, you'll find out what this website is about and what the service provides. They don't tell you how to sign up or join immediately, and they allow you to peruse the site on your own.
