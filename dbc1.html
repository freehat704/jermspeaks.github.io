Why Dev Bootcamp?

A bit of background. I graduated with a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering from Cal Poly and have been working in the medical device field for a year and a half. Specifically, I've been in manufacturing for minimally invasive devices, or devices that do not involve large incisions, e.g. needles and catheters.


I am joining Dev Bootcamp because I really want to learn to code in an environment where I can get constant feedback of where I can continue to improve upon. I've tried coding on my own, but without proper guidence or feedback, it was difficult to keep that up.

Coding history

  • I created my first website when I was 12. It was with Geocities.
  • I wrote a bunch of scripts to make tagging music a lot easier in my late teens
  • I spent a considerable time in college creating models and simulations using technical computing languages. I also learned basic C language and how to use the command prompt for UNIX computers.


What is Jeremy's Philosophy?

Live simply, and work to live. The basic three tenets I live by is socializing, creating, and learning. With this basis, I am formulating and implementing many projects, including a novel, art piece, and a business.


I abide my my own rules, which is just stolen or "well-adapted" from our great thinkers. I value in my own personal philosophy the following things: critical thought, kindness and care to others, respect, finding meaningful purpose in what I do, as well as what I say, keeping myself balanced, and to expand my knowledge.
